The Community Wellness Program at Supraja Hospital is dedicated To promoting healthier lives through preventive care, early screening, health education, and accessible medical support for all age groups. Our program conducts regular health camps, awareness drives, lifestyle counselling sessions, and chronic disease screenings to help the community stay informed and proactive about their well-being. With a focus on prevention, early detection, and continuous support, Supraja Hospital aims to build a healthier, stronger community through compassionate and comprehensive wellness initiatives. The Community Wellness Program at Supraja Hospitals in Nagole, Hyderabad, serves as a vital public health initiative designed to bridge the gap between advanced clinical care and preventative community health. Strategically targeting local neighborhoods and surrounding suburban areas, the hospital frequently organizes large-scale free health screening camps at public venues, corporate spaces, and major regional landmarks—such as their mass medical camp at the Yadadri Temple where hundreds of devotees received free checkups and consultations. These outreach programs focus heavily on early disease detection, offering subsidized or completely free diagnostic testing for high-risk conditions like hypertension, diabetes, cardiac irregularities, and women’s health issues. Beyond static screenings, Supraja Hospitals proactively elevates public safety by hosting hands-on clinical training, including foundational Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) workshops for regional police departments and local community leaders. By blending these educational awareness campaigns with affordable, structured preventative health packages, the program actively fosters a proactive health culture, aiming to reduce emergency hospitalizations through early intervention and accessible lifestyle education.
